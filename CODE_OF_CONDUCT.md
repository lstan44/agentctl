# Code of Conduct

agentctl is for people building capable systems under explicit trust boundaries.
The project community should work the same way.

## Expected conduct

- Be direct, respectful, and specific.
- Critique claims and implementations, not people.
- Prefer reproducible evidence to status or certainty theater.
- Protect private configuration, credentials, and user data.
- Make room for disagreement across tools, models, and engineering traditions.

## Unacceptable conduct

Harassment, discrimination, threats, doxxing, deliberate intimidation, sexualized
attention, and knowingly unsafe disclosure are not accepted in project spaces.

## Reporting and enforcement

For ordinary conduct concerns, contact the maintainers through a private channel
listed on the maintainer's GitHub profile. For security-sensitive reports, use
[private vulnerability reporting](https://github.com/lstan44/agentctl/security/advisories/new).

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, lock discussions, or temporarily
or permanently restrict participation when conduct puts people or the project at
risk. Reports will be handled as privately and promptly as practical.

This policy applies in project repositories, discussions, issue trackers, and
events where someone represents agentctl or justREPL.
